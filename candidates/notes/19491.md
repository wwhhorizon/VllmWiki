## Why not promoted
Closed stale issue with no direct linked fix in local evidence, but the symptom is too central to skip: `AsyncLLM` under enough concurrent pressure produces changed logprobs and sometimes changed outputs even when reproducibility guidance is followed. This deserves continued tracking as a verification / reproducibility boundary.

## Evidence summary
The report says identical prompts under `AsyncLLM` can yield different logprobs and occasionally different outputs, especially under larger concurrent batches. That makes it a stronger reproducibility signal than many generic “temperature=0 drift” issues, because it already narrows the suspect surface toward async scheduling / concurrency rather than plain kernel noise.
